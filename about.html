History

Bethel Mental Wellbeing was established in 2023 with a mission to transform attitudes towards mental health and disability in Western Kenya, particularly in the Marama North Ward of Kakamega County. We are dedicated to raising awareness and providing psychosocial support to individuals facing mental health challenges and disabilities. These individuals have often been marginalized and isolated due to cultural stigmas that perceive them as outcasts within their communities.

In many parts of Western Kenya, individuals with mental health challenges or disabilities face significant stigma and discrimination. These individuals are often abandoned and viewed as outcasts, leading to their isolation and neglect. Families of these individuals are frequently misunderstood and sometimes accused of engaging in cult activities or taboo practices, believed to cause their relatives' conditions. Such misconceptions drive families to hide their loved ones with disabilities, often keeping them in seclusion within their homes.Bethel Mental Wellbeing recognized the need to address these challenges head-on, aiming to change perceptions and improve the quality of life for individuals with mental health issues and disabilities. that is why we saw the need to come and solve this community challenge. This C.B.O is under a church Hossana Community Church Centre.

Bethel Mental Wellbeing being a C.B.O for persons with mental challenges and disabilities has helped people to accept others with mental disabilities and reach out places or organisations to help them lead a healthy life and economically empowered through education and trainings and employement.
